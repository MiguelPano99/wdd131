A Glimpse into Macau's Rich Past

Macau's history is a captivating tale of trade, cultural exchange, and colonial influence, spanning over 400 years.

Early Settlement

Before the arrival of the Portuguese, Macau was a sparsely populated fishing village, occasionally used by Chinese merchants.

Portuguese Arrival and Trade (16th-19th Century)

In the mid-16th century, Portuguese traders settled in Macau, establishing it as a major trading post between China, Japan, and Europe. This period saw the construction of many iconic churches and fortifications.

20th Century and Handover

The 20th century brought significant changes, including economic growth and, ultimately, the handover of Macau to China on December 20, 1999, becoming a Special Administrative Region.
