About Mazatlán

Nestled on Mexico's stunning Pacific coast, Mazatlán is a vibrant city renowned for its golden beaches, rich history, and lively culture. From the historic charm of its Old Town (Centro Histórico) with its neoclassical architecture and lively plazas, to the modern allure of its Golden Zone (Zona Dorada) with bustling resorts and nightlife, Mazatlán offers a diverse array of experiences for every traveler. Enjoy delicious seafood, exhilarating water sports, and breathtaking sunsets that paint the sky in fiery hues.

Key Attractions

Historic Old Town (Centro Histórico)

Wander through cobblestone streets, admire colonial buildings, and visit the beautiful Immaculate Conception Basilica. Explore art galleries, boutiques, and enjoy live music in Plazuela Machado.

Mazatlán Malecón

Stroll along one of the longest boardwalks in the world, offering stunning ocean views, iconic monuments, and a vibrant atmosphere perfect for walking, jogging, or cycling.

Golden Zone (Zona Dorada)

Experience the bustling heart of tourism with luxury resorts, fine dining, lively bars, and endless shopping opportunities. Perfect for sunbathing and water sports.

Stone Island (Isla de la Piedra)

A serene escape just a short boat ride away. Enjoy pristine beaches, horseback riding, ATV tours, and delicious fresh seafood in a laid-back setting.

Upcoming Events & Festivities

Mazatlán is famous for its vibrant cultural calendar. Don't miss these highlights!

Mazatlán Carnaval

One of the largest and most spectacular carnivals in the world, featuring parades, music, fireworks, and non-stop celebrations. (Typically in February or March)

Day of the Dead Celebrations

Experience the beautiful and poignant traditions of Día de Muertos with altars, parades, and cultural events throughout the city. (November 1-2)

First Friday Art Walk

Explore the vibrant art scene of Centro Histórico as galleries open their doors for an evening of art, music, and wine. (First Friday of each month)
